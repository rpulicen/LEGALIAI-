N-400 Documents Checklist — Everything You Need for Citizenship

Missing even one document can delay your citizenship application by months. This complete checklist tells you exactly what to gather, where to get it, and why USCIS needs it.

GET YOUR PERSONALIZED LIST — $49

Before you submit your N-400, gather every document on this list. USCIS will review these at your interview. Missing documents don't just delay your application — they can trigger a request for evidence (RFE) that adds months to your wait time.

💡 IMPORTANT: Always send copies — never originals — unless USCIS specifically requests them. Keep all originals in a safe place at home.

REQUIRED FOR ALL APPLICANTS

01
Permanent Resident Card (Green Card)
WHERE TO GET IT: Your physical card. Make front and back copies.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Proves your immigration status and when you became a permanent resident. This is the most important document.
02
Passport(s) from Your Birth Country
WHERE TO GET IT: Your current and any expired passports from the past 5 years. Contact your home country's embassy if you need a replacement.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Confirms your identity, nationality, and travel history. The passport stamps show every time you left the U.S.
03
Federal Tax Returns — Last 5 Years
WHERE TO GET IT: Download from IRS.gov (Get Transcript), or ask your tax preparer. You need Form 1040 for each year.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Proves continuous U.S. residence and that you filed taxes as required. Missing tax returns are a red flag.
04
Travel Records — All Trips Outside the U.S.
WHERE TO GET IT: Review your passport stamps. Create a list with: destination, departure date, return date, total days outside U.S.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Documents your continuous residence. Trips over 6 months can affect your eligibility. Be thorough — include even short weekend trips.
05
Two Passport-Style Photos
WHERE TO GET IT: Any post office, CVS, Walgreens, or FedEx. Must be taken within the last 30 days. Cost: $10–15.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Required for your naturalization certificate. Specific size and format requirements — ask the photographer for "USCIS passport photos."
06
Filing Fee — $725
WHERE TO GET IT: Pay by check, money order, or online at USCIS.gov. Made payable to "U.S. Department of Homeland Security."
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Covers the $640 application fee + $85 biometrics fee. Fee waivers available if you qualify based on income.

IF MARRIED TO A U.S. CITIZEN (3-Year Rule)

07
Marriage Certificate
WHERE TO GET IT: Vital records office in the county/country where you married. Allow 2–4 weeks for delivery.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Proves your marriage to a U.S. citizen, which qualifies you for the 3-year residency rule instead of 5 years.
08
Proof of Spouse's U.S. Citizenship
WHERE TO GET IT: Copy of spouse's U.S. passport, birth certificate, or naturalization certificate.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Confirms your spouse is actually a U.S. citizen, not just a permanent resident.
09
Proof You Live Together
WHERE TO GET IT: Joint bank statements, joint lease or mortgage, joint utility bills, or joint tax returns showing same address.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Confirms you are in a bona fide marriage living together, not just legally married on paper.

IF PREVIOUSLY MARRIED

10
Divorce Decree or Death Certificate
WHERE TO GET IT: Court records where the divorce was finalized, or vital records for death certificate.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Every prior marriage must be accounted for. USCIS needs to see how each previous marriage ended.

IF YOU HAVE CRIMINAL HISTORY

⚠ CRITICAL: If you have any arrests, citations, or criminal charges — even minor ones, even if charges were dropped — you must disclose them. USCIS will find them. Hiding past legal issues is grounds for denial and can affect future applications.

11
Court Disposition Records
WHERE TO GET IT: The court where your case was heard. Request "certified disposition records."
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Shows the outcome of any arrest or charge. Even if charges were dismissed, you need the paperwork showing that.

IF YOU SERVED IN THE U.S. MILITARY

12
DD-214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge)
WHERE TO GET IT: Request at archives.gov/veterans. Allow 3–6 months. Military service may qualify you for expedited naturalization.
WHY USCIS NEEDS IT: Verifies honorable service. Veterans may qualify for naturalization without the residency requirement.

DOCUMENT ORGANIZATION TIPS

Use a binder with labeled tabs. Organize documents in the same order as your N-400 sections. This makes the interview much smoother.

Make 3 copies of everything. One set for USCIS, one for your records, one as backup. Never submit originals unless required.

Get translations certified. Any document not in English must have a certified translation. A bilingual family member is NOT sufficient — use a professional translator.

⚠ Don't wait until the last minute. Some documents like IRS transcripts and court records can take 2–8 weeks to arrive. Start gathering now, even if your application is weeks away.
