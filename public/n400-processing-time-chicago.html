HOW TO CONFIRM THIS IS YOUR OFFICE

Your field office is determined by your home address when you file — not where you work or were born. Verify yours:

01
Office Locator: egov.uscis.gov/office-locator — enter your zip code to confirm your assigned office
02
Your receipt notice: Form I-797C shows your assigned field office name once you've filed
03
USCIS Processing Times Tool: egov.uscis.gov/processing-times — select N-400 and your office for the current verified figure

HOW TO CHECK YOUR CASE STATUS

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

I moved to a different area — does my field office change? +
If you move after filing, update your address with USCIS within 10 days using your online account or Form AR-11. Your case may transfer to the field office covering your new address. There is no guarantee of transfer, but updating your address is required by law regardless.
Can I speed up processing at my field office? +
USCIS does not accept general expedite requests for N-400 applications. The most effective way to avoid delays is to file a complete, error-free application, attend all appointments as scheduled, and respond immediately to any USCIS requests. An RFE can add 3-6 months to your timeline regardless of office.
What happens at the citizenship interview? +
The USCIS officer reviews your N-400 application answers, tests your English reading and writing, and administers the civics test. Under the updated 2026 format, the officer asks up to 20 questions from a pool of 128 — you must answer at least 12 correctly. The interview typically takes 20-45 minutes for prepared applicants.
