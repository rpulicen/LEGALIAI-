COMMON N-400 HELP QUESTIONS

How do I start my N-400 application?
Confirm your eligibility, then file online at my.uscis.gov ($710) or by mail ($760). Full step-by-step guide →
What documents do I need for N-400?
Green card, all passports, 5 years of tax returns, travel record, birth certificate, and more depending on your situation. Full documents checklist →
How long does the N-400 process take?
8 to 26 months depending on your USCIS field office. National median is 7.8 months. Full timeline guide →
What happens at the citizenship interview?
English test, civics test, and a review of your N-400 application. Takes 20–45 minutes. Full interview prep →
What if I have a criminal record?
Disclose everything — non-disclosure is far more damaging than the underlying issue. LEGALIAI walks through every Part 12 question. Good moral character guide →
What if I traveled outside the U.S. for a long time?
Any single trip of 6+ months requires additional documentation. Your continuous residence and physical presence calculations matter. LEGALIAI checks both.
What if I already received an RFE?
You have 87 days to respond. An RFE is not a denial. Full RFE response guide →
What if my application was denied?
You have 30 days to appeal with Form N-336. A denial is not permanent. Full denial guide →
